Chapter 11

Sustainable management of transport systems

Impact of developments of transport systems

Impact on natural environment
- Clearing of natural vegetation to obtain physical space - Road development can divide forests into smaller fragments - Reduces the size of animal habitats - Reduces the movement of animals - Deaths of animals trying to cross busy roads - Wildlife corridors - Alteration of natural landforms (rivers, mountains, slopes etc.)

Carbon Footprint

Definition: Measure of greenhouse gases emitted to support human activities Fossil fuels - Motorised transport - Generation of electricity for electricity-powered transport - Growth of cities (more transportation built) - Transport amounts for 25-30% of greenhouse gas emissions

Mobility

Definition: ability of people and freight to move from place to place - Important as it allows people access to goods and services - Facilitates social interactions for our emotional well-being

Physical capabilities & health-related conditions

Elderly Persons with disabilities (PwD)
More time & support needed to move around
  • Long term intellectual, physical or sensory impairments
  • Hindering them from carrying out daily activities
    • Prevents equal access to opportunities

Silver Zones

→ Areas within residential neighbourhoods with enhanced customised road safety features for the elderly
Key considerations for silver zones
  1. High proportion of senior citizens
  2. High accident rates involving seniors
  3. Close proximites to amenities frequrented by seniors

Traffic congestion

Definition

Happens when road usage approaches or exceeds road capacity

Characteristics
  • Slow travelling speeds
  • Vehicular queues / build-up of vehicles
Concentration of traffic congestion in cities
  • CBD / commercial centres where businesses are located
  • Areas with high pedestrian and vehicular flow
  • During peak hours
    • Morning, evening
  • Roads connected/near the CBD

Impacts of traffic congestion

Pollution
  • Large amounts of traffic causes traffic congestion
    • Traffic congestion increases pollution in a city
    • Increases carbon footprint which worsens air quality
Types of pollution
Stress on physical health
  • Loss of concentration
  • Falling asleep
  • Asthma and lung disease from pollution emitted by traffic
Stress on mental health
  • Stress, frustration and anxiety
  • Makes drivers more likely to get involved in fights and accident
Air pollution Smog – ‘a combination of smoke and fog, caused by exhaust and particles from cars and factories.’
Negative effects of smog:
  • Difficulty in breathing
  • Asthma
  • Eye irritation
  • Higher risk of lung cancer
Noise pollution
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Reduce work performance
    • Mental stress
  • Hearing impairment
  • Can trigger heart-related illness and risks

Negative impact of transport

  • Increase demand for healthcare and insurance due to serious injuries
  • Loss of lives and physical disabilities from serious accidents
  • Damage to both personal and public properties

Personal safety

  • Increases risk of crime on public transport
    • theft
    • outrage of modesty

National security

  • Terminals/major nodes may become targets of terrorist attacks

Sustainable management of transport systems

1. Managing people’s demands

  • Car-lite policies
    • Discourage private vehicle ownership (COE)
    • Encourage commute via walking, cycling and taking public transport --> reduce carbon emmissions
2. Managing road usage
Features
  • Adjusted from time to time
  • Drivers pay more during peak hours
  • Discourages drivers from using certain roads
Disadvantages
  • Traffic is diverted elsewhere (but still exists)
  • Regulations have to be tightly enforced
Examples
  1. Electronic Road Pricing (ERP)
    • Higher rates for entering during peak hours
    • Higher rates for entering CBD
  2. Stockholm Pricing System
    • Applies to vehicles entering and exiting the CBD
    • Cameras at control points capture trips made by vehicles
    • Bill is sent to the owner of car (at the end of every month &c.)
Results (Advantages)>
  • Bill is sent to the owner of car (at the end of every month &c.)
  • Traffic delay decreased by 30-35%
3. Integrated land-use and transport planning
Integrated land-use Transport planning
  • How and where the land should be used
  • Shape the demand for travel and distance needed to travel
  • Forecast for present and future needs:
    • Operation and provision of transport
    • Management of infrastructure
Requisites

Requires close co-ordination and communication between different stakeholders
to balance social, economic and transport needs, so that there is a balance in social-economic and transport needs.

4. Land transport master plan (LTMP)
Key Idea
Long-term plan to develop a transport network that is convenient, well-connected and fast. Aims
  • Develop inclusive infrastructure
  • Promote gracious behaviour
  • Enable safer journeys on public transport
Develop:
  • 20-minute towns
  • 45-minute cities
Using:
  • public
  • active
  • shared nodes
To:
  • Neighbourhood centres
  • Across the city
Results (Advantages) Good planning ensures that:
Transport facilities and services are able to move people and goods in a:
  • safe
  • affordable
  • convenient
  • environmentally-friendly
manner.

Disadvantages
