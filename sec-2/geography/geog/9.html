The definition of Sustainability

Definition of "sustainable"

  1. able to be maintained at a certain rate or level
  2. able to be upheld / defended

Definition of "sustainable housing"

  1. ensure housing does not affect the environent and resources so that future generations can still enjoy the benefits of them
  2. housing that is able to provide for the people and last for a long time

Effects of the usage of natural resources

Deforestation

Definition: forests are cleared to make space for urban populations as cities grow
Results in:
  • lesser trees to take in carbon dioxide
      • --> CO2 in the atmosphere --> global warming
  • loss of bio-diversity
  • loss of food sources
  • loss of plants with medicinal values
Other natural resources used extensively:
  • Fuel – burnt for cooking/warmth
  • Timber – used to make furniture
  • Natural Gas – burnt to produce electricity
  • Steel – building material to reinforce houses
  • Water – used for drinking and sanitation

Water-stress

Too much water usage can cause cities to be water-stressed

Pollution

Definition: introduction of substances that do not normally belong to the environment
Land Pollution Land pollution consists of rubbish and industrial waste, and soil contamination

Rubbish and industrial waste:
  • mining
  • industry
  • offices
  • homes
  • schools
Soil contamination
  • factory chemicals
  • sewage
  • waste water
Management techniques:
  • land-fills and incinerator plants
Benefits of incinerating rubbish:
  • reduce space needed in land-fills
  • generate electricity
  • remove odours

Water pollution (A subset of land pollution)

Causes:
  • washing, bathing, and defecating in water sources
  • agro-chemicals frm agriculture being washed onto water bodies
  • surface runoff from streets, causing contaminants from vehicles and other sources to be washed into rivers
  • dumping of untreated waste from factories, industries
  • fuel leakage
Ways to manage:
  • proper waste disposal
  • usage of natural pesticides and fertilisers

Air Pollution

Causes:
  • burning of fossil fuels for electricity and energy
  • emissions from industries and factories during production (must write!)
  • emissions from motor vehicles
Ways to manage:
  • monitoring outdoor air quality, and acknowledge the negative impacts (of air pollution) on people's health
  • increase ridership of public transport
  • increase usage of alternative, cleaner energy sources such as solar panels

General idea

Higher concentration of people = higher concentration of waste = higher concentration of pollution
