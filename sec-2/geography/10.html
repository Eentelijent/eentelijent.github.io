Transport systems and their spatial distribution

I. What are transport systems?



Movements → rely on transport networks
Transport Networks → nodes linked by routes
Nodes → location that provides entry to a transport network
Route →path for travel between nodes

*More nodes + routes = denser network
*Urban areas have a denser network compared to rural areas
  • Easier for people to travel between locations
  • Possible for people to be more independent
  • Easier for peole to participate in different activites

Importance of transport

  • It connects people between different locations
  • It creates opportunities for social conversations and activities,
  • It creates opportunities for economic activities

Quality of transport infrastructure


Indicator
 Measurable examples
Coverage
 Easily reached:
  • Percentage of population living within 1 km of the transport node?

Capacity
 Must be able to carry large amount of people or goods
  • How many passengers can be picked up at 1 station?
  • How many trucks can use 1 unloading bay at the same time?
Environmental sustainability
 Must not affect air quality or environment
  • What is the environmental impact of the infrastructure? (air quality?)
Convenience
 Must be barrier free and easy to transfer from one mode to another
  • Is access barrier-free?
  • How easy is it to pay for a trip/parking? (possible costs? cards?)
  • How easy is it to transfer from 1 transport mode to another?
Frequency
 Must not have long waiting times
  • What is the distance between the current and next transport service?
  • Frequency of maintenance

II. Where are transport nodes located in a city?

Nodes (at different locations)

  • Major �  area with high-transport density
    • Terminal � facility where passengers / goods start/terminate
    • Interchange � facility where passengers can transfer within/between different transport networks
  • Minor
    • Bus station
    • MRT station

Concentration of activities

  • Cities have many activities
    • banking
    • manufacturing
    • education
    • leisure
  • Transport nodes are often located at areas with activities so that more people can access them
Common areas with high concentration of activites
  • CBD
  • Tourist areas (e.g. little india)

Accessibility

Definition: the ease of reaching destinations or activities

Higher cost = lower accessibility
Higher travelling time = lower accessibility
 --> Major transport nodes such as interchanges are usually located in areas with high accessibility

III. Why are transport systems located in cities

Movement of people

  • Separation of workplace and home
    • people need to travel to and from their homes to their work places daily
    • Access to local social activities and amenities
      • people need to participate in social activities as to improve their social and emotional well-being

Movement of goods and services

  • Supply goods and services (from manufacturers to consumers)
    • rise of e-commerce
    • increased demand for door-to-door services
  • Supply people's daily needs & essential services
    • tank trucks deliver petroleum to petrol kiosk
    • water trucks deliver water to homes during droughts
    • poop trucks in burj khalifa (google it)

    If the time taken for the transportation to transport goods to another area is longer, then the cost is lowe

Type of network (connection between cities)

Point-to-point network: allows people to travel between cities directly
Hub-and-spoke network: allows people to travel between cities via transfer at a hub.\

  1. Point-to-point Network
    1. Direct connection Between locations
    2. Movements are limited → transport is limited to only between 2 points

  2. Hub and Spoke Network
    1. Allow people to travel through a transfer at a hub (e.g. Changi Airport)
    2. More types of movement →  can transit at different intermodal points
