Security challenges Singapore faced

Konfrontasi

President Sukarno
1st Indonesian President (1945-1967)
  • Sukarno was facing an economic crisis and needed something as a distraction
  • Sukarno opposed formation of Malaysia
    • Believed it would give british more control in the region
    • Sukarno wanted to increase indo power in region
    • Sukarno wanted to control the whole of Borneo

Timeframe: 1963-1966
Macdonald house bombing
  • 3 killed, 33 injured
  • Singapore killed the 2 soldiers involved → led to strained relations between indo and sg
  • Was only fixed when LKY scattered flowers on the 2 soldier's graves

Communist Terrorism

Despite the split of the MCP into 2 major rival factions + the end of the malayan emergency, many communists continued a guerilla war
  • 1970 - Killing 7 year-old in changi
  • 1974 - Bomb explosion in katong
  • 1976 - Attempted assassinaton of commissioner of police

International Terrorism

  • Foreign terrorists (guess, commies) hijacked the ferry boat Laju
  • Terrorists tried to attack a shell oil refinery on pulau bukom on sg
  • An agreement was made for the terrorists to get safe passage to kuwait
  • First major encounter with international terrorism

Viet nam War

  • Worried that fighting would spill over and destabilise south-east asia
  • communism

British military withdrawal from Singapore

  • The British could no longer afford to maintain such a large force in SEA
  • Withdrawal was announced in 1967, most left by 1971, and completed in 1976

State of Singapore's military
  • Singapore only had 2 battalions of 2,000 men
  • Force was too small to defend against anything --> sg was unable to defend itself

Methods used by Singapore to overcome its security challenges

  • Deterrence
  • Diplomacy

Deterrence

Reasons for using deterrence
  • Make enemies reconsider invasion of Singapore
  • Defend Singapore in case deterrence failed
  • Manage internal security
National Service
  • Conscription was introduced in 1967
  • Created a large manpower pool which could build a credible defence force
  • So that other countries would not be tempted to invade singapore
Establishment of a local defence industry
  • the Chartered Industries of Singapore (CIS) was founded to produce arms for sg
  • Production of licensed M16S1 rifle
  • Trained own scientists and engineers to design arms for sg
  • Allowed singapore to produce its own arms even when foreign sources were cut off
    • So that Singapore could continue to fight despite blockade
    • So that Singapore can design technology more suitable for sg's needs
Strengthening military co-operation with other countries
Creation of the Five Power Defemce Arrangement (FPDA) with:
  • Singapore
  • Malaysia
  • New Zealand
  • Australia
  • United Kingdom
→ committed the 5 members to consult each other should sg/malaysia be invaded
Reasons for using diplomacy
  • Ensure the recognition of Singapore as an independent state
  • To ensure that singapore is safe
Growing bi-lateral relations
Bilateral → between 2 countries Multilateral → between multiple countries
  • Scattering of flowers on the 2 soldiers' grave in indo
  • Visit to great wall of china
→ Ensure many countries, even those that did not take a direct interest in singapore, to recognise and stand up for it
Increase co-operation so other countries won't invade as it is not lucrstive (?)
Joining regional/international organisations
  • Joining UN – 21 September 1965
  • Founding of ASEAN – 1967
    • Got Vietnam to withdraw from Cambodia in 1978
→ Ensure that Singapore had allies and supporters
