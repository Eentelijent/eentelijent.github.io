Why were there riots in the early 1950s?

Maria Hertogh Riots (1950)

Cause A perceived bias towards Europeans by colonial authorities
People Involved
  • Che Aminah (caregiver)
  • Maria Hertogh (the main character)
  • Adeline Hertogh (biological mother)
Impact British lost support from muslim communities
Main Points
  • Maria was placed in a Catholic Convent despite her being a muslim
  • The case was sensationalised and portrayed as an issue between Islam and Christianity
  • It is a custody battle over the girl, maria hertogh and her custody between 2 mothers, che aminah and adeline hertogh

Anti-NS Riots (1953)

Cause
  • British announcement of NS Ordinance Law in 1956
  • Chinese-medium school students angered
    • Their education had been affected by WWII
    • British gave very little support and resources to chinese-medium schools
  • Students felt that the British did not doenough for them and yet wanted them to do “defend a foreign nation for nothing in return” (TB page 55)
People involved
  • Chinese-medium, secondary school students
  • Chinese community
  • British colonial authorities
Impact
  • British lost support of chinese
    • British already contributed less to chinese-medium schools
    • Chinese more vocal in politics
      • Formation of SCMSSU (Singapore Chinese Middle School Students’ Union)

Rendel Constitution

Aim Find out how to appease the local people (how the local people could play a more active role in government)
Significance Paved way for self-government in 1959
Implications New constitution → Elections to be held in 1955
  • The 1955 elections generated more interest and political activity than the 1948 and 1951 elections
People’s Action Party (人民行动党)
Labour Front (Winner)
  • Both were pro-independence and anti-colonial
  • Both campaigned for worker’s rights
    • Context: frequent strikes back in 1950s
Results:
The Labor Front won the election

Hock Lee Bus Riots (1955)

Cause SBWU - Started a strike for better and fairer conditions compared to the Hock Lee Bus Employees’ Union
(HLABC) - Fired 229 workers in response
People involved
  • Singapore Bus Workers’ Union (SBWU)
  • Hock Lee Amalgamated Bus Company (HLABC)
    (to a lesser extent)
  • Employees’ Union
Impact Impact on David Marshall’s Government:
British Impression:
  • Seemed weak and incapable
    • Marshal did not take a hard-line stance but instead chose to negotiate
    • UK had great fears of communists taking control
People’s Impression
  • Suspicious of government
  • Continued to organise and protest

1st Merderka Talks

Aim Negotiate for self-government
Outcome Did not succeed
  • David Marshall steps down as chief minister
  • Lim Yew Hock takes over

  • British doubtful of capability of LF to maintain law and order
    • British needed strong and stable government in Singapore
    • Threat of communism to British interests → British did not want Singapore to turn communist

Chinese Middle School Student Riots

Cause Singapore Chinese Middle School Students’ Union (SCMSSU) was banned and 142 chinese middle school students expelled on alleged communist activities
People Involved
  • Chinese middle school students
  • Police and LF government
 Impact Impact on Lim Yew Hock’s Government

British Impression
  • Pleased with tough action and anti-communist stance

People's Impression
  • Saw Lim Yew Hocks’ government as ‘running dogs’ of the British (a person over-eager to serve his master)

Main Points:
  • Lim Yew Hock was pro-british government so his government was more harsh to 'communist activity'
  • Anti-colonial sentiment led to formations of unions and strikes
  • The Chinese were especially displeased with British government
    • influence from mao ze dong's victory in the chinese civil war
    • british government favoured english-medium schools over chinese ones
  • The SCMSSU were banned due to alleged communist activities

2nd Merderka Talks

Aim Negotiate for full internal self-government
  • Success
    • A new constitution was to be written
    • Gave Singapore control of all domestic issues except internal security except for:
      • Defence
      • Diplomacy
    • Elections to be held in 1959
  • Decline in LF popularity
    • Lim Yew Hock was seen as overly pro-British and too harsh
    • Chinese voters didn't like how lim yew hock settled the chinese middle school student riots
  • More interest compared to 1959 elections
  • Citizen ordinance made 320,000 more people eligible to vote
  • LF became the Singapore People's Alliance (SPA) after a merger
