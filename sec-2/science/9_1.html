Chapter 9 (I)

Applications of forces

Forces in nature

  • climate change --> tropical cyclones
  • sliding of earth's tectonic plates --> earthquakes
  • magma under the earth under high pressure --> volcanic eruptions
  • underwater earthquakes and volcanic eruptions tsunamis

Forces

Definition: force is exerted when pushing/pulling an object
SI Unit: Newtons (N)

Types of forces:

Contact: Friction
Non-contact: Magnetic, Gravitational

Frictional Force

Definition: A force that opposes motion at the surfaces in contact
  • friction acts in opposition to applied force
  • the arrow used to represent friction must be drawn at the point of contact

Reducing Friction

Friction can be reduced by:
  • having a streamlined shape
  • having a smooth body that reduces water and air resistance
  • applying lubricants such as grease and oil to the surfaces in contact
  • attaching ball-bearings to the surfaces in contact
  • preventing or reducing the area in contact between 2 objects
    • maglev trains → levitation via magnets
    • hovercraft → “floats” on water on a cushion of air

Magnetic Force

Definition: The force of attraction or repulsion produced by magnets.

Usages:

  • sorting magnetic metals in scrapyards
  • magnetic seal in refrigerator doors

Gravitational Force

Definition: The force of gravity between two bodies

Usages:

Effects of interactions between two objects and forces

Forces interact with object to produce certain effects. A force can:
  • change the shape and size of certain objects
  • change the state of movement of a body
  • change the direction of a moving object
  • change the speed of an object
  • cause objects to pivot

Turning effect of a force

  • known as a pivot
  • can be clockwise or anti-clockwise
  • this effect is knowns as the moment of the force.

Pressure

Definition: the force per unit area
SI Unit: Pascals (Pa)
a unit for pressure is also "newton-metre", since the formula is pressure = newtons/area in metres-square.

Formula

P = F / A

P = pressure (pascals),
F = force (newtons),
A = area (m-sq),
