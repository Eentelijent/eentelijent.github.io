Co-valent bonding

What is covalent bonding?

Covalent bonds are formed when atoms share electrons between them.
Covalent bonds are drawn with the dot-and-cross diagram.

Rules for drawing covalent bonding diagrams

  • × and ⬤ must be aligned to the direction of parent (attatched) atom
  • If no atoms can achieve a full valence shell each, a total of at least 8 valence electrons will do
  • Dots-and-crosses always come in pairs

Examples

Water (oxygen + 2 hydrogen)

Ammonia (nitrogen + 3 hydrogen)
