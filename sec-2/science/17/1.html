Ionic Bonding

What are ions?

Definition:

positively or negatively charged particles.

How are ions formed?

Ions are formed when atoms lose or gain electrons.
Loses electron → positive ion (cation)
Gains electron → negative ion (anion)

Why are ions formed?

Atoms of elements react to achieve the 'noble gas structure.'.
To do this, atoms need to have a full outermost shell.

When are ions formed?

Metal reacts with non-metal

What holds ionic compounds together?

Electro-static force which forms an ionic bond.

Noble Gases

Noble gases, the Group VIII (Group 18) elements have the following properties:
  • They are all gases
  • They are extremely unreactive
    • DO NOT combine with other elements
List of noble gases
  • Helium
  • Neon
  • Argon
  • Krypton
  • Xenon
  • Radon
* the right-most 'column' (group)
* all noble gases end with a -on prefix (except for helium, which wasn't known to be a noble gas when first discovered)
Why noble gases are so unreactive
All of these atoms have a full outermost shells of electrons.
