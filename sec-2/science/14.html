Food tests

Iodine food test

Test for: Starch
Add a few drops of iodine to sample solution to be tested
Positive: Iodine changes from brown/yellow to blue-black
Negative: Iodine solution remains brown

Benedict's Test

Test for: Reducing Sugars
  • Add 2cm3 of benedict’s solution to 2cm3 of sample solution
  • Shake to mix
  • Heat in water bath (boiling water)

Positive: Brick-red precipitate
Negative: Soultion remains blue

Biuret Test

Test for: Proteins
  • Add 2cm3 of sodium hydroxide solution to 2cm3 of sample solution to be tested
  • Add 1% copper (II) sulphate solution drop-by-drop
Positive: Violet colouration is observed
Negative: Solution remains blue

Ethanol Emulsion Test

Test for: Reducing Sugars
  • Add 2cm3 of ethanol to be tested
  • Shake mixture thoroughly
  • Add 2cm3 of water to the mixture
Positive: a white emulsion is seen
Negative: mixture remains colourless
  • Most food are too large to be absorbed by cells directly, as the plasma-cell surface membrance (C.S.R.) is partially permeable
  • Large, insoluble food moleques must be broken down into smaller, soluble molecues to be absorbed
Definition of digestion
The breakdown of large insoluble food molecues into small, soluble (diffusible) food molecues that can be absorbed and used by the body

The role of enzymes

Definition: Enzymes are biological catalysts that alter the rate of chemical reactions in our bodies without being changed and are specific in action Characteristics of enzymes
  • Digestive enzymes increase the rate of digestion
  • Enzymes only work with specific molecues and cannot be used for other types
  • Enzymes digest food into small molecues

Role of different organs in the digestive system

Mouth and windpipe



Mouth Œsophagus
Teeth: Cut and grind food into smaller pieces
Saliva: Mucus that softens food
Tongue: Mixes food with saliva and rolls food

Saliva:

  • Contains salivary amylase
  • Speeds up chemical breakdown of starch into maltose
    • Maltose tastes slightly sweet
  • A narrow muscular tube
  • Food moves via peristalsis
  • Amylase continues to chemically breakdown starch, but no enzymes are secreted here

Peristalsis
  • Food here is called bolus
Eplglottis
  • Flap that covers windpipe

Accesory organs and stomach

Stomach
Definition: Bag-like region with gastric glands that produces acids and enzymes
  • Acids
    • Acid – kills bacteria and parasites
    • Provides a suitable environment for enzymes
  • Enzymes
    • Protease – speeds up the breakdown of proteins into polypeptides
  • Food that leaves the stomach after digestion is called chyme
Accessory Organs
  • Liver
  • Gall bladder
  • Pancreas

Lower alimentary canal



Small intestine Large intestine Rectum et Anus
  • Approx. 6 metres long
  • Digestive enzymes are secreted
  • Digested food molecues are absorbed here

Function

  • Absorbs food
  • Small molecues of digested food can enter the bloodstream
  • The length of the small intestine allows for a higher rate of absorbtion

Villi
  • Finger-like protrutions on the walls of the small intestine
  • Provides a large surface area for absorption of food
 Function
  • Absorbs water and mineral salts

Features
  • Undigested materials become a semi-solid waste
  • Undigested materials become faeces (shit, essentially)
Rectum
Function
  • Faeces is temporarily stored here
Anus
  • End of the alimentary canal
  • Faeces is passed out here via a process known as egestion
