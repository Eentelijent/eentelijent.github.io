Digestive system

Mouth and windpipe

Mouth
The mouth is the first organ in the alimentary canal.
Teeth: Cuts and grinds food into smaller pieces
Saliva: Mucus that softens food
Tongue: Mixes food with saliva and rolls food into a ball

Saliva
Saliva is a liquid produced by the salivary glands.
  • Contains salivary amylase
  • Speeds down chemical breakdown of starch into maltose
  • Food mixed with saliva is called bolus
Oesophagus
A narrow, muscular tube that moves food to the stomach.
  • Food moves down the oesophagus via peristalsis
  • Amylase continues to chemically break down starch into maltose.
    • Note: NO SALIVARY AMYLASE IS PRODUCED HERE
Peristalsis

Muscles along the oesophagus contract and relax to push bolus down.

Epiglottis

A flap that covers the windpipe.
