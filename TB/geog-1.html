Chapter 1

Summary

Learning Overview

In this topic, the following is taught:

  • Relationships between people and nature in their neighbourhoods

Key Points

  • Local communities and nearby nature areas are:
    • Dependent upon each other
    • Mutually affect each other
  • Nature brings benefits to people by:
    • Removing pollution
    • Lowering temperatures
    • Providing spaces for recreation
  • People bring benefits to nature by:
    • Causing soil erosion and damaging vegetation
    • Worsening pollution
    • Disturbing wildlife
  • Nature may bring disadvantages to people as:
    • Wildlife attacks can harm oeioke
    • Environmental pollution can limit development(s)

Content concepts

  • Community — A group of people in a particular place who have a sense of belonging both to the place and to their group.
  • Nature Area — The natural environment; refers to the non-human world, which are areas that are untouched/barely affected by humans.
  • Recreation: — Any voluntary activity undertaken for purposes of enjoyment, pleasure, and personal satisfaction.
  • Neighbourhood — A sub-division in an urban area that is distinguished by some character or quality (mental attatchment), where residents share commonalities.
  • Wildlife — Animals of a wild nature, including their young or eggs.
  • Vegetation — The collection of plant life that provides ground cover in a region.
  • Environmental protection — Any activity to protect, conserve abd restonre nature habitats and (their) species.
  • Soil erosion — Soil particles being detatched, transported and deposited by water, wind, gravity, animals, or plants.
  • Pollutant — Substances that are present in concentrations that may harm humans, plants and animals.
  • Pollution — The introduction of harmful substances into the environment.

What is the relationship between people and nature?

  1. Local communities and nearby nature areas are dependent upon each other.
    • People are dependent on nature for many things:
      • Nature provides essentials to living such as air and water
      • Living organisms like bees pollinate crops for a continuous supply of food
      • Nature provides spaces for recreation and leisurely activites
        • Allows people to bond with their family and friends

          • This allows humans to maintain their mental well-being
        • People can maintain their physical well-being
